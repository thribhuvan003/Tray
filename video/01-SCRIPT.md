### Mic technique (quick + free)
- **Distance:** 4–6 inches (a fist) off the mic, slightly off-axis to avoid plosives. Use a pop filter, or angle the mic ~30° off your mouth.
- **Room:** Record in a soft space — closet with clothes, or duvet over your head and the mic. Kills echo for free.
- **Levels:** Aim peaks around −6 dBFS; never clip. Record one clean "room tone" (5s of silence) for noise removal.
- **Delivery:** Stand up, smile slightly on Beat 4 (the reveal), keep water nearby, and do 2–3 takes per line — pick best, comp later.
- **Edit (free):** Audacity → Noise Reduction (using the room tone) → gentle compression → normalize to −3 dB → high-pass filter at ~80 Hz to cut rumble.

### Free TTS fallback (if you don't want to record your own voice)
- **Microsoft Edge "Read aloud" / edge-tts (free):** voices `en-US-GuyNeural` (warm male) or `en-IN-PrabhatNeural` / `en-IN-NeerjaNeural` (Indian English — fits the campus context). CLI: `pip install edge-tts` then `edge-tts --voice en-IN-PrabhatNeural --text "..." --write-media line.mp3`.
- Spell tricky tokens phonetically in the TTS input: write `"U P I"`, `"O T P"`, `"rupees"`, `"RAH zor pay"`, `"tri-bhoo-van"` so the engine pronounces them right.
- Other free options: ElevenLabs free tier (limited chars, very natural), or Google TTS via `gtts` (robotic but fine for a scratch track).
- Whichever you use: render each line separately so you can re-time to the beat map without re-rendering the whole VO.
