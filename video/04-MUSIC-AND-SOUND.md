# TRAY — Music & Sound Design Spec

**Deliverable 04 · Composer & Sound Designer**
Project: TRAY — "Shopify for campus canteens" (multi-tenant canteen management for colleges)
Format: Hackathon submission video, 2:00, cartoon explainer + real product screen recordings, for judges
Creator: Thribhuvan (solo)
Tone: emotional + a little witty + technically credible
One-liner: *"Tray — Shopify for campus canteens: 0% commission, no code, and the lunch line disappears."*

Emotional spine: a ticking-clock lunchtime countdown ("12:47, thirteen minutes to class"). Signature line: *"A samosa is not worth a debarred semester."* Resolution: the chaotic canteen line dissolves into a calm queue.

---

## 0. THE "TRAY THEME" MOTIF (read this first)

Everything in the score orbits one short, hummable idea. It must be recognizable in 3 seconds, work both anxious (minor) and hopeful (major), and survive being played on a single instrument.

- **Motif (4 notes, the "Tray hook"):** scale degrees **5 – 1 – 2 – 3** (rising), a gentle "lift up and resolve" shape. Think of it as the musical version of a tray being lifted and set down satisfied.
  - In **A minor** (tension version, used 0:00–0:36): E – A – B – C, played sparse, single instrument, slightly hesitant.
  - In **C major** (resolve version, used at 0:38 DROP and 1:40 RESOLVE): G – C – D – E, full and warm. A minor and C major share notes, so the SAME motif flips from anxious to hopeful with zero retuning — that's the emotional trick the whole video rides on.
- **Rhythmic signature:** the first note lands slightly before the beat (pickup), then the last three land ON the beat — this is what makes it feel like it "arrives."
- **Three states the motif appears in:**
  1. **Fragment / question** (HOOK + STAKES): only the first 2 notes, unresolved, on a music box or lone plucked synth.
  2. **Statement / answer** (DROP 0:38): full 4 notes, major, lead synth + warm pad — this is the version judges remember.
  3. **Anthem / payoff** (RESOLVE 1:40 + CLOSE): full 4 notes, harmonized, with the demo's percussion underneath, ending on a held tonic chord.
- **Tempo home base:** 120 BPM (so 0:38 DROP, 0:52 DEMO and 1:40 RESOLVE all share a common pulse and edits cut cleanly between them). Hook/stakes sit at a slower implied feel via half-time.

**Drop notes (0:38):** The 2 seconds of near-silence at 0:36 are doing the work — do not score over them. The whoosh is the only sound bridging the gap, and it should crescendo so the downbeat at 0:38 feels inevitable. Land the first full drum hit + bass note + the major motif on the exact frame the "Shopify for campus canteens" line/visual appears.

**Pull-back notes (1:24):** Genuine contrast = genuine impact. Kill the music almost entirely (leave only a low sub rumble or nothing). The "0%" lands as a single resonant hit (coin-drop SFX + a low piano/sub note). Silence here makes the number feel important and gives the claim gravitas. Resist filling the space.

**Resolve notes (1:40):** This is the emotional mirror of the DROP but bigger and warmer — same motif, now harmonized and confident, because the story has been "earned." Where the DROP was a surprise, the RESOLVE is a return home. Add a higher octave of the motif and a sustained string bed to lift it past where the demo peaked.

## 3. MIX & MASTER NOTES

**Hierarchy: dialogue-led.** VO is the star; music and SFX support it. Build the mix VO-first, then SFX, then music last (music fills the gaps, it doesn't compete).

- **Loudness target:** **-14 LUFS integrated** (YouTube/web normalization standard). Short-term should not exceed ~-9 LUFS even in the DROP.
- **True-peak ceiling:** **-1.0 dBTP** (use a true-peak limiter; -1.5 dBTP if any further re-encoding/upload transcoding is expected).
- **VO level:** average around -12 to -10 dBFS, peaks near -6 dBFS, compressed gently (2:1–3:1, ~2–4 dB GR) for consistency. De-ess and high-pass at ~80 Hz.
- **Music ducking (sidechain):** sidechain-compress the music bus to the VO bus. **Duck music ~4–6 dB** when VO is present; **fast attack (~5–10 ms), release ~250–400 ms** so music breathes back between phrases. During VO-free sections (0:36–0:38 build, the 0:52 demo grooves between lines, 1:40 resolve), let music return to full.
- **SFX bus:** keep hero SFX (UPI ting, coin-drop, vault thunk) ~3–6 dB above the music bed so they cut through; do NOT sidechain hero stingers to VO (they should punch). Light reverb on the logo sting only.
- **Stinger handling:** the 0:38 drop hit, the 1:24 "0%" hit, and the 1:52 logo sting are transient-heavy — let them peak briefly toward -1 dBTP but tame with the master limiter, not by lowering them (they're meant to feel loud).
- **Where to leave silence (do not fill):**
  - **0:36–0:38** — the held breath before the drop. Near-silence + only the whoosh.
  - **1:24** — the pull-back. Near-silence around the "0%" coin-drop. This silence IS the gravitas.
  - **Final ~0.5s** — let the logo sting's reverb tail decay to true silence rather than a hard cut.
- **Frequency carving:** high-pass the music slightly (~120 Hz shelf -2 dB) under VO so low-mids don't muddy the voice; reserve sub (<60 Hz) for the drop, the "0%" hit, and the vault thunk only.
- **Stereo:** VO mono-center; music wide; SFX panned to picture but hero stingers center.

## SUMMARY

The whole score hangs on ONE 4-note motif (rising 5-1-2-3) that flips from anxious A-minor in the hook to warm C-major at the 0:38 DROP and again at the 1:40 RESOLVE — same notes, opposite feeling, which is the emotional payoff judges remember. Everything runs at 120 BPM so the demo's UI taps and the two big reveals cut cleanly together. The two most important sounds are the *silences*: the held breath at 0:36 before the drop, and the near-silent pull-back at 1:24 where a single coin-drop sells "0%." Mix dialogue-led at -14 LUFS / -1 dBTP with ~4–6 dB of sidechain ducking under the VO, and let hero stingers (UPI ting, coin-drop, vault thunk, logo sting) punch through un-ducked. Source from Pixabay/YouTube Audio Library/Uppbeat (+ AI prompts above), recreate — never rip — branded UPI chimes, and keep a licenses.txt for anything CC-BY.
